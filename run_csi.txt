RUN CSI.M

After you place csi.m somewhere on your MATLAB path and open your CSI bundle:
open("/home/obak-b/rx_4_251203_030315.csi");
data = rx_4_251203_030315;
You can run:
    • Full CSI report
      csi(data, 'summary');
      Shows: NumTx, NumRx, CBW, carrier frequency, sampling rate (derived from Timestamp), NumTones, etc., plus heuristic AP/STA MACs.
    • Visualization toolkit
      csi(data, 'plot');                 % default first ~200 frames
      csi(data, 'plot', 'frames', 1:50); % custom frame subset
      Plots:
        ◦ amplitude vs subcarrier (single frame)
        ◦ phase vs subcarrier (single frame)
        ◦ amplitude heatmap (frames × tones)
        ◦ phase heatmap
    • Subcarrier frequency mapping
      csi(data, 'subcarriers');
      Prints a table of SubcarrierIndex → frequency offset (MHz) and optional mean-amplitude-vs-frequency plot.
    • Export for Python
      csi(data, 'export', 'basename', 'my_csi');
      Produces:
        ◦ my_csi.mat (H, Amp, Phase, subcarriers, ts_sec)
        ◦ my_csi_H.npy (complex CSI as [N, K, 2] real/imag)
        ◦ my_csi_Amp.npy
        ◦ my_csi_Phase.npy
    • Heuristic AP / STA identification
      csi(data, 'identify_ap_sta');
      Uses frequency of MACs in Addr3 (BSSID) and Addr1/Addr2 to guess AP vs STA.
You can open csi.m to tweak anything (e.g., plots, export fields), but it should work out of the box with the PicoScenes structure you showed.
